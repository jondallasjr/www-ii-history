WWII Timeline: Major Events 1939-1945

A chronological overview of critical World War II events

September 1, 1939

Germany Invades Poland

Germany launches Operation Wei√ü, invading Poland and marking the official start of World War II in Europe. Britain and France declare war on Germany two days later.

Military Europe

May-June 1940

Fall of France

Germany invades France and the Low Countries. France surrenders on June 22, 1940. The Dunkirk evacuation saves over 300,000 Allied troops.

Military Europe

July-October 1940

Battle of Britain

The German Luftwaffe attempts to gain air superiority over Britain in preparation for an invasion. The Royal Air Force successfully defends, and Hitler abandons invasion plans.

Military Europe

June 22, 1941

Operation Barbarossa

Germany launches a massive invasion of the Soviet Union with over 3 million troops. This becomes the largest military operation in history and marks a turning point in the European theater.

Military Europe

December 7, 1941

Attack on Pearl Harbor

Japan attacks the U.S. naval base at Pearl Harbor, Hawaii, killing over 1,100 soldiers and destroying numerous ships. The United States declares war on Japan the next day.

Military Pacific

October-November 1942

Battle of El Alamein

British forces under General Bernard Montgomery defeat German and Italian forces in North Africa, halting Axis expansion and marking the beginning of the Allied offensive in Africa.

Military North Africa

August-February 1943

Battle of Stalingrad

A brutal urban battle between German and Soviet forces in Stalingrad. The Soviet victory marks a major turning point on the Eastern Front, with Germany beginning its long retreat.

Military Europe

February-March 1943

Battle of the Bismarck Sea

Allied forces defeat a major Japanese convoy attempt to reinforce the Solomon Islands, marking a critical point in the Pacific island-hopping campaign.

Military Pacific

June 6, 1944

D-Day: Normandy Invasion

Operation Overlord launches with over 150,000 Allied troops landing on the beaches of Normandy, France. This marks the beginning of the liberation of Western Europe from Nazi occupation.

Military Europe

May 7-8, 1945

Germany Surrenders

Germany unconditionally surrenders to the Allies. General Dwight Eisenhower accepts the surrender on May 7, marking Victory in Europe (V-E) Day.

Military Europe

August 6 & 9, 1945

Atomic Bombings

The United States drops atomic bombs on Hiroshima and Nagasaki. The devastation kills over 200,000 people and forces Japan to surrender.

Military Technology Pacific

September 2, 1945

Japan Surrenders

Japan formally surrenders aboard the USS Missouri in Tokyo Bay, officially ending World War II. This day is celebrated as Victory over Japan (V-J) Day.

Military Pacific
