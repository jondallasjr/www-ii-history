About This Site

Our Mission & Approach to WWII History

Our Mission

The WWII History site is dedicated to providing comprehensive, accurate, and accessible information about World War II. We aim to educate students, researchers, educators, and history enthusiasts about the causes, events, key figures, and lasting impact of this pivotal moment in human history.

Our mission is to:

  • Present historical facts with scholarly rigor and clarity
  • Make complex historical events understandable for diverse audiences
  • Preserve and share primary sources from the era
  • Honor the experiences and sacrifices of those who lived through WWII
  • Foster critical thinking about history and its lessons for today

Editorial Standards

We maintain rigorous editorial standards to ensure the accuracy and reliability of all content on this site:

Scholarly Research

All content is based on peer-reviewed historical scholarship, primary sources, and established academic works on WWII.

Multiple Perspectives

We present diverse viewpoints and acknowledge different interpretations of historical events while maintaining factual accuracy.

Transparent Sources

We provide citations and reference materials, allowing users to verify information and explore topics further.

Regular Updates

Content is reviewed and updated regularly to reflect current historical scholarship and correct any identified inaccuracies.

Our Contributors

This site was developed by a team of historians, educators, researchers, and history enthusiasts committed to making WWII history accessible to everyone. Our contributors include:

  • Academic Historians: Specialists in military history, European history, and WWII studies
  • Educators: Teachers and instructional designers who understand how to present complex information clearly
  • Researchers: Scholars working with primary sources and archival materials
  • Subject Matter Experts: Specialists in specific battles, campaigns, and historical periods

Our Approach

We believe that understanding WWII requires:

Historical Context

Understanding the causes and conditions that led to World War II, including post-WWI politics, economic factors, and ideological movements.

Human Stories

Recognizing that history is made by people—soldiers, civilians, leaders, and ordinary individuals whose choices shaped events.

Global Perspective

Covering the war across all theaters—Europe, the Pacific, North Africa, Asia—and including voices from all nations involved.

Educational Value

Presenting information in ways that help learners at all levels—from high school students to advanced researchers.

Our Commitment to Accuracy

Historical accuracy is our paramount concern. We:

  • Verify all facts against established historical records and scholarly sources
  • Correct errors promptly when they are identified
  • Distinguish between established facts, scholarly interpretations, and areas of historical debate
  • Avoid presentism—judging past events by today's standards without historical context
  • Acknowledge gaps in the historical record and areas where evidence is incomplete
  • Treat all subjects, including difficult topics like the Holocaust, with appropriate sensitivity and respect

Have Questions or Suggestions?

We value feedback and are committed to continuous improvement. If you notice any inaccuracies, have suggestions for additional content, or would like to contribute to this project, please contact us.
